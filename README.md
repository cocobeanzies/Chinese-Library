<h2 style="color: seashell;" align="center"> Important Note on Tones</h2>
<div align="center">
	<p style="color: seashell;" align="center">
	    Tone 1: <b>_</b>
		    <br>
		Tone 2: <b>/</b>
		    <br>
		Tone 3: <b>v</b>
		    <br>
		Tone 4: <b>\</b>
		    <br><br>
		<b>Tone 1 ( - )</b> = <i>"The Singing Tone".</i> Keep at your level.
		    <br>
        <b>Tone 2 ( / )</b> = <i>"The Unsure Tone".</i> Like you're asking a question. "Yes?"
		    <br>
        <b>Tone 3 ( v )</b> = <i>"Full Third Tone"</i> or <i>"The Zombie Tone".</i> Full Third Tone is the full accentuation that you only say if the word is by itself. Otherwise you use The Zombie Tone, where your voice goes down and fizzles out at the bottom like a zombie. "Braains..."
		    <br>
        <b>Tone 4 ( \ )</b> = <i>"The Assertive Tone".</i> Like you're scolding something. "No."
		    <br>
        <b>Tone 5</b> = <i>"The Contrarian Tone".</i> No symbols above the letters. Very short, snappy and neutral.
	</p>
</div>

<button class="collapse" align="center">
	Additional note ä¸€
</button>
    <p class="content" align="center">
		Tones are incredibly important in Chinese, as they symbolise the pitch of your voice whenever you say a word.
		    <br>
		Without tones, it can sound like you're saying something entirely different and confuse Chinese people.
		    <br><br>
		If you cannot type the tone, you should include a number after the word to signify what tone you meant.
		    <br>
		Even in typing, not using tones is incredibly confusing.
	</p>
